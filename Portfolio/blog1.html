About spotify

Spotify is a service that provides streaming music and content (DRM-protected) from Sony, EMI, Warner Music Group and Universal Music Group. Music can be searched by artist, album, genre, playlist or label. The subscription, or "Premium" service, allows users to remove ads and download music offline for listening. The service was launched by the Swedish company Spotify AB in October 2008. As of September 15, 2010, the service had approximately 10 million users, including 2.5 million users who subscribed to the service.[6][7] In December 2012, Spotify reached 20 million users, of which 5 million paid for the service. In January 2015, Spotify reached 60 million users, 15 million of whom paid for the service. Spotify became available in Croatia on July 14, 2020. In late February 2021, hundreds of k-pop songs were removed from the platform due to disagreements with South Korean music distributor Kakao M.

Availability

As of January 2015, Spotify is available on the following platforms: Android, BlackBerry, Boxee, iOS, MeeGo, Windows, Openpandora, macOS, Roku, S60 (Symbian), Samsung Smart TV, Squeezebox, Telia Digital-tv, TiVo , WD TV, webOS, Windows Mobile and Windows Phone. The service does not have an official app for Windows 8, but there is a Spotlite client made by independent developers and available in the Windows Store. Spotify worldwide availability. More about spotify

With Spotify, it’s easy to find the right music or podcast for every moment – on your phone, your computer, your tablet and more. There are millions of tracks and episodes on Spotify. So whether you’re behind the wheel, working out, partying or relaxing, the right music or podcast is always at your fingertips. Choose what you want to listen to, or let Spotify surprise you. You can also browse through the collections of friends, artists, and celebrities, or create a radio station and just sit back.
