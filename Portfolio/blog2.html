Blog 2 - HDD vs SSD

Best Choice

Hard disk drives (HDD) have been reliable for decades data storage devices. They are usually lower prices than SSDs and are available in larger capacities. Their biggest advantage is the ability to store a lot of data cheaply. However, moving parts, vibrations and high temperatures were always a potential problem data storage device. They are usually lower prices than SSDs and are available in larger capacities. They still use HDDs big institutions like banks, government because is the price per GB lower than the price of SSD. When you have deal with huge amounts of price data quickly becomes an important factor.

Solid-state drive or SSD for short is a device to store the data it uses for storage integrated circuits. It is intended to be permanent data storage, as well as the hard disk. Although is sometimes referred to as an "SSD drive", it does not contain mechanical parts (read/write head and magnetized disc). SSD technology for communication with the rest of the computer uses the same electronic interface as well as hard drives, which facilitates the installation and replacement of slower mechanical ones disks. Due to higher writing and reading speed, a new SATA input/output interface was constructed Express to make the rest of the computer circuit followed and made the most of new technology.

WHAT TO CHOOSE - HDD OR SSD?

Deciding which is the better choice for you, it comes down to the way you use your computer. SSD (Solid State Disks) and HDD (Hard Drive Disk) they are similar in physical specifications, but the way on which they store data is completely different.
