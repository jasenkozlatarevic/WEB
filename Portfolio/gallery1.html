Ahmediya or Sultan Ahmed Mosque is the Turkish state mosque and landmark of Istanbul, the largest Turkish city and the former capital of the Ottoman Empire (from 1453 to 1923).
The picture was taken while I was in Istanbul.

Lloret de Mar is a small Catalan tourist town located on the Mediterranean coast in the province of Girona and one of the most popular tourist destinations on the Costa Brava. It is located near the cities of Tosa de Mar and Girona, it is only 75 km from Barcelona. The main beach in Lloret de Mar, 1,630 meters long, is one of the most popular in the Costa Brava and has been awarded the Blue Flag for cleanliness. What is most popular in Lloret is its nightlife, which attracts tens of thousands of 18- to 30-year-olds from all over Europe.

Sagrada Familia picture taken during graduation excursion to Spain, In 1866, the bookseller Bocabella founded a religious society dedicated to Saint Joseph, which in 1881 bought land for a basilica in the outer district of Barcelona, ​​Barrio del Poblet. The construction of the basilica was originally entrusted to the architects Martorello and Francesco de Paulo Villar, but after the construction of the crypt, they invited the young and promising architect Gaudí (who was then 31 years old).

Ahmediya Mosque The mosque began to be built on the prestigious site of the former Constantine's Palace, overlooking the Hagia Sophia (then the most visited mosque in Istanbul) and the Hippodrome. A large part of the southern side of the mosque continues to the foundations and vaults of the basement of Constantine's palace. Several palaces, which already existed in that place, had to be bought (at a great price) and demolished (like the palace of Mehmed Pasha Sokolović).

Bremen The Free Hanseatic City of Bremen is a city in northwestern Germany and at the same time the administrative center of the smallest federal state of Bremen. Bremen has 548,477 inhabitants and lies on the Weser River, 95 km southwest of Hamburg, 70 km from the North Sea and 53 km from Bremerhaven. It is an important road and railway junction and the airport.
The picture was taken during my stay in the hotel in Bremen in 2021

The Philips Stadion is a football stadium in Eindhoven, Netherlands, and it is the home of PSV Eindhoven (Philips Sport Vereniging), and therefore also known as PSV Stadion. With a capacity of 36,500, it is the third-largest football stadium in the country. Established as the Philips Sportpark, it was constructed as a sports field for Philips employees in 1910. The Philips Elftal played football matches from 1911 until 1913, when the team was succeeded by PSV. Since 1913, PSV has used the original ground as its home stadium. The stadium has gone through several extensions in its history: after a wooden stand in 1916, seating and standing places slowly enclosed the field throughout the first decades.
The picture was taken during my stay in Eindhoven in 2021

Amsterdam is the capital and an important economic and cultural center of the Netherlands. It is located at the mouth of the Amstel River in the IJsselmeer, North Holland. The wider metropolitan area has more than 1.36 million inhabitants, which makes it the sixth largest metropolis in Europe.
The picture was taken in 2021 during the period when I was in this Dutch city

Hagia Sophia officially the Great Holy Mosque Hagia Sophia is a mosque, a former Orthodox church and a museum in Istanbul. It was originally built by order of the Byzantine Emperor Justinian I the Great from 532 to 537 and was the largest church in the world.In 1453, after the fall of Constantinople, it was turned into a mosque. From 1934 to 2020, it served as a museum. On July 10, 2020, by revoking the decision from 1934, it was declared a mosque again.
The picture was taken this summer during a tour of Istanbul
